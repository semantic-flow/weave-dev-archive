## Structure

- IRI permanence and application-sustaining availability need a technical make-over
  - learn the static site lesson
  - use the most robust hosting platforms
    - purl is ok, but as the archive.org DOS proved, leave the hard work of availability to the pros
  - don't require customized HTTP codes to differentiate sense vs content or request headers to negotiate content
- single referent principle: identifiers that point to digital artifacts should always refer to the same thing
  - but data often needs to be updated and "identifier sense" can evolve.
  - solution is to support terminology that allows you to be as specific or general as warrented
  - RDF needs a consistent way to differentiate between an artifact in general, different snapshots of an artifact over time, particular byte-arrangements of an artifact (manifestations), and likely retrieval locations.
  - if an identifier needs to split its senses, you can mint a new one; or better, mint to new ones and leave a trail
- Additionally, Semantic Flow attempts to address a twenty-year-old conundrum known as httpRange-14: does a given IRI identify content; or do they identity things, or the "sense" of a thing.
